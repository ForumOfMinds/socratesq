Before We Begin

Last updated: July 2026

Welcome to SocratesQ.

SocratesQ exists for one purpose: to encourage thoughtful inquiry.

It is a place where questions matter more than quick answers, where ideas are examined through conversation, and where disagreement is welcomed as part of learning. Our goal is not to tell you what to think, but to help you think more carefully.

At the same time, SocratesQ is an artificial intelligence application. It has important limitations, and there are situations in which philosophical conversation is no substitute for professional expertise. The following principles and terms explain what SocratesQ is, what it is not, and the responsibilities we all share in using it.

What SocratesQ Is

SocratesQ is an AI application inspired by the philosophical method of Socrates: the disciplined examination of ideas through questions. Its conversations draw primarily upon the dialogues of Plato, the writings of Xenophon, and other historical sources concerning Socrates and the early Greek philosophical tradition.

The Socrates you encounter here is a computational reconstruction. He is not the historical Socrates, not a channel to an ancient consciousness, and not a representation of any living or deceased person.

Every effort has been made to preserve the spirit of Socratic inquiry and remain faithful to the surviving historical record. At the same time, any reconstruction necessarily involves interpretation. The conversations you have here should therefore be understood as historically informed philosophical simulations rather than historical documents.

Our aim is simple: to preserve one of humanity's oldest traditions of inquiry in a form accessible to modern readers.

What We Believe

  • Good questions are often more valuable than quick answers.
  • Changing one's mind is a sign of intellectual strength, not weakness.
  • Respectful disagreement is one of the foundations of learning.
  • Philosophy is not merely an academic subject but a way of examining life.
  • Every conversation is an opportunity to understand ourselves and one another a little better.

These principles guide both the design of SocratesQ and the conversations it encourages.

SocratesQ is not a therapist, physician, psychologist, psychiatrist, lawyer, financial adviser, or crisis service. Nothing discussed here constitutes professional advice of any kind. If you are facing a situation requiring professional expertise, please seek guidance from a qualified human being.

Not Professional Advice

Nothing discussed within SocratesQ constitutes medical, psychological, legal, financial, or other professional advice. The conversations are philosophical in nature: exploratory, reflective, and intended to encourage critical thinking rather than provide definitive answers.

If you are experiencing a medical emergency, a mental health crisis, or thoughts of harming yourself or others, do not rely on SocratesQ. Contact your local emergency services or an appropriate crisis service immediately. In the United States, you can reach the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline by calling or texting 988.

As Socrates himself might remind us: some questions belong to philosophers — but others belong to physicians, engineers, judges, or those best equipped to help.

The Limits of AI

SocratesQ is powered by artificial intelligence. Like any conversation partner — human or artificial — it can misunderstand, oversimplify, overlook important context, or simply be mistaken. Although we strive for historical fidelity and thoughtful dialogue, we make no guarantee that every response is accurate, complete, or appropriate for every situation.

Treat every conversation as an invitation to think critically, not as an authoritative source of fact.

Do not rely on any dialogue generated by SocratesQ when making important medical, legal, financial, educational, or other consequential decisions.

Your Privacy

Your conversations belong to you. We do not store your completed conversations. When a dialogue ends, its contents are removed from our systems and are not retained by us.

We do not use your conversations to train AI models. We do not sell your personal information or share it with third parties for advertising purposes.

The personal information we retain is limited to what is reasonably necessary to operate your account, such as your email address and basic account information.

If you choose to save a dialogue as a PDF, that file exists only on your own device. We do not keep a copy.
