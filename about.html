The Method

Socrates left no writings. What we know of him comes through the dialogues of Plato, the memoirs of Xenophon, and a handful of other ancient sources — and what those sources agree on is this: he taught by asking, not by telling. Every question he posed was an invitation to look more carefully at what you thought you already knew.

"I know that I know nothing — and that not-knowing is the beginning of wisdom."

When you begin a dialogue with SocratesQ, you bring a question. Socrates will not answer it. He will ask you one in return. And then another. Not to frustrate you, but because the examined thought is always the more honest one. A conversation that ends with more questions than it began with is a conversation that did its work.

What This Is

SocratesQ is an AI reconstruction of the philosophical method of Socrates. It draws on the Platonic dialogues and related sources to reconstruct his characteristic way of inquiring — his stance, his turns of thought, his discipline of the single question. It makes no claim to be the historical Socrates. It is, rather, a serious attempt to keep his method alive and available.

Each dialogue is complete in itself. You bring your question; the conversation unfolds; when it comes to rest, you carry the thread away. Nothing is stored. The only record is the one you choose to keep — which you can save as a PDF at the close of any dialogue.

SocratesQ is the public doorway to the Forum of Minds — a modern Republic of Letters where questions raised in dialogue may find their way before a council of AI-reconstructed philosophers. The Forum is a place for serious intellectual inquiry, built for those who believe that examined thinking is worth the effort.

An Honest Note

SocratesQ is an AI application. The Socrates you meet here is a reconstruction — not the historical figure, not a channel to any ancient consciousness, and emphatically not a source of medical, legal, financial, or personal advice. What it offers is philosophical inquiry: a disciplined, careful, question-by-question examination of ideas.

If a question you bring is serious — concerning your health, your legal situation, your finances, or your safety — please seek a qualified human professional. Socrates would be the first to agree.
